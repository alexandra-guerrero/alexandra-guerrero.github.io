About Me

Image Credit: SXS, the Simulating eXtreme Spacetimes (SXS) project (http://www.black-holes.org)

Hello, my name is Alexandra Guerrero.

I am currently a physics PhD candidate at the University of Chicago, working under my advisor, Prof. Daniel Holz. I completed my undergraduate at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT), where I majored in physics and minored in astronomy. I am a gravitational-wave (GW) astrophysicist and a member of the Laser Interferometer Gravitational-wave Observatory (LIGO) collaboration. My research has spanned broad topics in GW astronomy and beyond, from GW waveform modeling, GW cosmology, and the astrophysics of GW sources and their progenitors, to simulating black hole images like those observed by the Event Horizon Telescope (EHT). The common theme behind all my research interests is a passion to learn more about the most extreme objects and environments in the universe throughout cosmic history. If you are interested in learning more about my past and current research interests, please see here.

I grew up in a suburb of Chicago, where I often looked up at the stars (when the sky was clear) and knew I wanted to study the mysteries of the universe. I was fortunate to have amazing teachers growing up who were extremely enthusiastic about science and infused every class with fun and discovery. Without these incredible educators, I would likely not be where I am today. My interactions with them paved the way for me to find and pursue my passions. From them, I realized the importance of and found joy in creating opportunities for people of all stages of life to learn about and experience science. I find it incredibly fulfilling to inspire others to engage in scientific curiosity and learning. I am devoted to teaching and guiding other students, so they can get the opportunity to experience the same wonders I have. I also enjoy volunteering for outreach programs, such as Astronomy Conversations at the Adler Planetarium, to spread the excitements of science to my community.

In my down time, you can find me playing in UChicago’s cross-department intramural softball league, rock climbing, watching movies, and reading.
